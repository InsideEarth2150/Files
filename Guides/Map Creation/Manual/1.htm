1. Creating a simple game layout

New Map
Click on "New" (it's on the right hand side of the status bar). This opens up a window where you can define general parameters.
You have the following options:

- Level name (under which the level will function in the game),

- File name (under which the layout will be saved on the hard disk),

- Map size (from the smallest - 32 x 32 - to the largest -256 x 256),

- Type of surface (on which the game will take place),

- Type of liquid on the map – with a choice of water or lava. Here you can also construct the harbor,

- Sea level is the last option in this window. Here you can set how high above sea level the game will be played.

Type of Terrain
After setting the general parameters, you can define the type of terrain by "bending" the basic flat playing area. This can be done manually, using the "Altitudes" tab, or you can use the built-in map generators located on the "Generator" tab.
To make the map look even more realistic, you can use the "Generate Noise" option. This will generate slight ripples in the surface.

Insert Textures
You can texture any game area manually using the various tools you’ll find under the "Textures" tab. If you want to let the program do it automatically for you, go to the "Generators" tab and use "Generate Textures".

Resources
Needed raw materials must be spread over the game area. To do this, first use the tab "Special", then the tab "Resources". Point to a location and left-click. The longer you hold down the mouse, the more materials will be distributed at that location. You’ll see two information pointers on this tab: the one on the left tells you the total amount of raw materials on the game area and the one on the right tells you the size of the currently highlighted vein.
The amount of raw materials in a particular field can be reduced by holding down the left hand mouse button and the SHIFT key simultaneously. "Brush Size" will expand the area in which the raw materials should be deposited. The area is displayed as a rectangle, from 1 to 7 fields in size.

Water/Lava
To flood a low-lying area with water, use the tab "Special", then click on "Water". The longer you left-click on the area, the higher the water level will rise. If the water level gets too high, you can lower it by pressing the left mouse button and the SHIFT key simultaneously.
Use this procedure with caution, otherwise too much land could be under water and that will hinder your own progress.

If you mistakenly flood too much land, use the "Undo Water" switch. This undoes the last action. But remember, you can only undo one time, i.e., one operation!

 

Important!!

The water model is extremely realistic, so much so that when you flood a strongly-contoured area (with hills, fissures) then lower the water or lava level, that water will run into fissures and low-lying areas and form small lakes!

Trees
Different types of trees make the game area much more realistic. First click on the "Special" tab, then on the 1 to 5 tree types on the Trees line. If you click on 1, 3 and 5, random selection of these particular tree types will follow. To remove trees and specify the area where you want to plant them, just do the same as you did in the earlier section, "Resources".

Stones
 

To create rocks, click on the "Special" tab then on the 1 to 5 rock types on the "Stones" line. Just use the same procedure as in "Resources" or "Trees".

Wrecks
 The Editor makes it possible for you to simulate a battlefield by introducing wrecks into an area. Use the "Special" tab, then the "Wrecks" line to choose wrecks for your particular race.

 

Bridges
 

A particular location will often be inaccessible. To reach a far shore, for example, you can build a bridge. Choose one of the 4 available types (using the "Special" and "Bridges" tabs, remember how to do it?) and select the bridge width. Remember, you can only use a bridge to reach really inaccessible points.

 

If you click on 1 to 4, you’ll see the 4 available bridgeheads. Left-click on one and drag it over to its opposite number. The location of the bridge will now be highlighted in green.

Several left clicks on the more distant bridgehead will then construct the bridge. You can’t build a bridge through a red field, however. There are different reasons for this, like an unsuitable water level, for example.

Tunnels
The game is played on two levels. The subterranean level is in your tunnels. You can’t construct anything here, but a tunnel can get you close to an enemy base or even get you right into the enemy base itself. Use the "Tunnels" tab and choose tunnel type and width. The types are listed in 4 columns. Each single column represents switches 1 to 16.

 

Each type of tunnel (Neutral, UCS, ED, Aliens) has 4 variations in the following groups: 1 - 4, 5 - 8, 9 - 12 and 13 -16. Each switch symbolizes a particular wall and floor version. Clicking on several, for example, 1, 2, 3 and 4, will give you a random distribution. There are 3 adjacent switches for selecting single, double and treble tunnel widths. In the next grup of switches are the following options:

1. "Show on Surface" – displays the path of the tunnel on the surface.

2. "Drag Tunnel" – highlights the tunnel’s path. Just left click and drag. Several left clicks will make the highlighted tunnel area appear.

3. "Put Rocks" – textures the game area with rocks, meaning you can’t dig a tunnel here!

Starting Points
 

When all the above items etc., have been placed on the game area, it’s time to determine the starting points, i.e., the point from which each player starts his/her particular game. You need to do this to be able to save the game later.

Saving the Game Map
 

So, you’ve done everything and are ready to start! Now save the Game Map by clicking on "Menu" and "Save". Now just type in the map name of your choice. This means you can now play Skirmish or Multiplayer. Good luck, and have lots of fun!!
